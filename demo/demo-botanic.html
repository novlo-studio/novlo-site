Our Philosophy

Where the forest
meets the table

Verdana Botanicals began as a kitchen experiment — a handful of dried herbs, a cold afternoon, and a curiosity about what plants could offer beyond food. That experiment became a practice, and that practice became a craft we share with our community.

Everything we make is small-batch, starting with thoughtfully sourced ingredients: local beeswax, organic cold-pressed oils, and dried botanicals from trusted growers in the Pacific Northwest.

We believe that slowness is an ingredient. That attention changes the character of what you make. Every bar, every blend, every small tin carries the care of hands that know the difference.

The Craft

Slow work, done well

01
🌿

Source & Select

We gather plants and materials from trusted growers — prioritizing organic, regenerative, and local where possible. Every ingredient earns its place.

02
🧪

Blend & Balance

Formulas are developed slowly, tested across seasons. We adjust for the weather, the batch, the way a particular harvest smells different from the last.

03
🤲

Craft by Hand

Every product is shaped, poured, pressed, or wrapped by hand in our studio. No automated lines. No shortcuts. Just presence and patience.

04
🌾

Cure & Finish

We let things rest. Soaps cure for eight weeks. Balms settle. Blends mature. Rushing is the one ingredient we never add.
